HOW TO READ THIS DOCUMENT
==========================================================================

PART 1 (ER summary) is the *what* — every entity, attribute,
relationship and constraint in one place. Use it as a reference
when drawing the ER diagram or building the relational schema.

PART 2 (assumptions log) is the *why* — every design decision
with reasoning. When PART 1 says "Hospitalization stores
base_cost AND extra_cost separately", PART 2 explains why
(see A23: enables Q1's clean SUM by category).

Each item in PART 1 has cross-references to assumption IDs
(e.g. "per A4"). When in doubt about a choice, look up the
assumption.

==========================================================
SECTION 1 — LOOKUP ENTITIES (small reference tables)
==========================================================
These hold fixed vocabularies. All have the same shape:
  (code or id PK, name, optional description).
They're not drawn prominently in the ER; usually shown as
small boxes off to the side or summarized in a legend.

  StaffType         — Doctor, Nurse, AdminStaff
  DoctorRank        — Resident, Curator B, Curator A, Director
  NurseRank         — Assistant Nurse, Nurse, Head Nurse
  AdminRole         — Secretary, Accountant, ... (extensible)
  Specialty         — Cardiology, Surgery, Internal Medicine,
                      Neurology, ...  (extensible)
  Gender            — M / F / O
  InsuranceProvider — ΕΦΚΑ, Private, Uninsured, ...
  BedType           — ICU, single, multi
  BedStatus         — available, occupied, under maintenance
  UrgencyLevel      — 1 (immediate) ... 5 (non-urgent)
  TriageOutcome     — discharged with instructions,
                      referred for hospitalization, ...
  ShiftSlot         — morning (07–15), afternoon (15–23),
                      night (23–07)
  ShiftStatus       — DRAFT, LOCKED
  ProcedureCategory — surgical, diagnostic, therapeutic
  RoomType          — operating theater, procedure room
  ExamType          — hematological, biochemical, imaging, ...

SECTION 2 — CORE ENTITIES (one by one)
==========================================================

----------------------------------------------------------
2.1  Staff  (ISA superclass)
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    PK  AMKA               — natural unique key
        first_name
        last_name
        date_of_birth      (per A4; age is derived)
      * age                (derived from date_of_birth)
        email
        phone
        hire_date
    FK  staff_type         → StaffType   (discriminator)

ISA: disjoint, total → {Doctor, Nurse, AdminStaff}
     (every Staff is exactly one of the three subclasses)

----------------------------------------------------------
2.2  Doctor  (subclass of Staff)
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes (in addition to inherited Staff attributes):
        license_number     — natural unique key
    FK  rank               → DoctorRank
    (specialties: M:N relationship — see 3.2)

Self-relationship: SUPERVISES
    Doctor (supervisor side) ─── (0,N) ─── SUPERVISES ───
                                  (0,1) ─── Doctor (supervisee side)
    A doctor has at most one supervisor; can supervise many.
    Business rules NOT in ER (enforced by triggers):
      - Resident MUST have a supervisor
      - Director MUST NOT have a supervisor
      - No cycles in the supervision chain

----------------------------------------------------------
2.3  Nurse  (subclass of Staff)
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    FK  rank               → NurseRank
    FK  department         → Department  (1:1 — exactly one)

----------------------------------------------------------
2.4  AdminStaff  (subclass of Staff)
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    FK  role               → AdminRole
        office             — work office (free text)
    FK  department         → Department  (1:1 — exactly one)

----------------------------------------------------------
2.5  Department
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    PK  dept_id            — surrogate
        name               — UNIQUE
        description
      * bed_count          (derived: COUNT of related Beds, A8)
        floor
        building

Relationships:
    MANAGES (Department ─── director ─── Doctor):  (1,1) ─── (0,1)
        Every department has exactly one director (a Doctor).
        A doctor manages at most one department.
    DOCTOR_WORKS_IN: (Department) (1,N) ─── (1,N) (Doctor)   M:N
    (NURSE_BELONGS_TO and ADMIN_BELONGS_TO already shown
     above as 1:N from Department to subclasses.)

----------------------------------------------------------
2.6  Bed   (weak entity, owner = Department)
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    PK (partial)  bed_number
        FK         department  (identifying)
    FK  bed_type           → BedType
    FK  status             → BedStatus

Identifying relationship HAS_BED:
    Department  (1,1) ─── HAS_BED ─── (1,N)  Bed
    Department's full PK contributes to Bed's identity.

----------------------------------------------------------
2.7  Patient
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    PK  AMKA               — natural unique key
        first_name
        last_name
        patronymic
        date_of_birth      (per A4)
      * age                (derived)
    FK  gender             → Gender
        weight             (kg)
        height             (cm)
        address
        phone
        email
        occupation
        nationality
    FK  insurance_provider → InsuranceProvider

Multi-valued: emergency contacts → modeled as own entity (2.8).

----------------------------------------------------------
2.8  EmergencyContact   (weak entity, owner = Patient)
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    PK (partial)  contact_seq    (1, 2, 3, ... per patient)
        FK         patient        (identifying)
        full_name
        relationship              (free text — mother, friend, ...)
        phone
        email                     (?  optional)

Identifying relationship HAS_EMERGENCY_CONTACT:
    Patient  (1,1) ─── HAS_EMERGENCY_CONTACT ─── (0,N)  EmergencyContact

----------------------------------------------------------
2.9  ActiveSubstance
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    PK  substance_id       — surrogate
        name               — UNIQUE (normalized at load, A17)

----------------------------------------------------------
2.10 Drug   (reference data — EMA Article 57)
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    PK  drug_id            — surrogate
        ema_code           — UNIQUE
        name
        manufacturer       (?)

Relationship CONTAINS:
    Drug (1,N) ─── CONTAINS ─── (1,N) ActiveSubstance
    M:N. Loaded by splitting EMA's '|'-separated field (A16).

----------------------------------------------------------
2.11 Triage
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    PK  triage_id          — surrogate
    FK  patient            → Patient
    FK  triage_nurse       → Nurse
        arrival_time       (DATETIME)
        triage_time        (DATETIME)
      * wait_time          (derived: triage_time − arrival_time)
    FK  urgency_level      → UrgencyLevel
        symptoms           (free text)
    FK  outcome            → TriageOutcome

----------------------------------------------------------
2.12 Hospitalization     (the central fact entity)
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    PK  hospitalization_id — surrogate
    FK  patient            → Patient
    FK  bed                → Bed
    FK  department         → Department
                             (denormalized but trigger-enforced
                             to match Bed.department, per A21)
    FK  triage             → Triage  (?, nullable)
        admission_date
        discharge_date     (?, nullable until discharged)
    FK  admission_diagnosis → ICD10
    FK  discharge_diagnosis → ICD10  (?, nullable)
    FK  ken_code            → KEN
        base_cost          (frozen at discharge)
        extra_cost         (surcharge for over-stay; ≥ 0)
      * total_cost         (derived: base_cost + extra_cost)

----------------------------------------------------------
2.13 ICD10   (reference data — Greek health ministry)
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    PK  icd10_code         — natural key (e.g. "I21.0")
        description
        category           (extracted prefix at load, A24)

----------------------------------------------------------
2.14 KEN     (reference data — closed unified hospital codes)
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    PK  ken_code           — natural key
        description
        base_cost
        avg_length_of_stay (ΜΔΝ, in days)
        daily_surcharge_rate
            (default = base_cost / avg_length_of_stay, A25)

----------------------------------------------------------
2.15 LabExam
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    PK  exam_id            — surrogate
    FK  hospitalization    → Hospitalization
    FK  ordered_by_doctor  → Doctor
    FK  exam_type          → ExamType
        exam_date
        result_text        (?)
        result_numeric     (?)
        result_unit        (?)
        cost
    CHECK: at least one of result_text / result_numeric is non-null (A26)

----------------------------------------------------------
2.16 MedicalProcedure   (reference data — catalog of types)
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    PK  procedure_code     — natural key
        name
    FK  category           → ProcedureCategory
        standard_duration  (minutes)
        standard_cost

----------------------------------------------------------
2.17 OperatingRoom
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    PK  room_id            — surrogate
        name               — UNIQUE
    FK  room_type          → RoomType
        floor              (?)
        building           (?)
    (Standalone — NOT tied to a Department, A29)

----------------------------------------------------------
2.18 ProcedureExecution   (a specific performance event)
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    PK  execution_id       — surrogate
    FK  hospitalization    → Hospitalization
    FK  procedure          → MedicalProcedure
    FK  room               → OperatingRoom  (?, optional —
                             only for procedures requiring a space)
    FK  main_surgeon       → Doctor
        start_time         (DATETIME)
        end_time           (DATETIME)
        actual_cost        (frozen, defaults to procedure.standard_cost)

Anti-conflict business rules (triggers, A31):
  - No two ProcedureExecutions in the same room overlap in time.
  - No doctor (as main_surgeon OR assistant) appears in two
    overlapping ProcedureExecutions.

----------------------------------------------------------
2.19 ProcedureAssistant   (associative — M:N)
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    PK  (procedure_execution, staff)  — composite
    FK  procedure_execution → ProcedureExecution
    FK  staff               → Staff (parent), CHECK type ∈ {Doctor, Nurse}
        role                (?, free text — anesthesiologist, scrub nurse)

----------------------------------------------------------
2.20 Prescription
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    PK  prescription_id    — surrogate
    FK  doctor             → Doctor
    FK  patient            → Patient
    FK  drug               → Drug
    FK  hospitalization    → Hospitalization (NOT NULL)
        dosage             (text — e.g. "500mg")
        frequency          (text — e.g. "every 8h")
        start_date
        end_date           (?, nullable for open-ended)

UNIQUE: (doctor, patient, drug, start_date)  — A35
CHECK:  end_date IS NULL OR end_date > start_date  — A38
TRIGGER: BEFORE INSERT — block if any of drug's active
         substances is in patient's allergy list (A37).

----------------------------------------------------------
2.21 Allergy   (M:N relationship: Patient ↔ ActiveSubstance)
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    PK  (patient, substance)  — composite
    FK  patient               → Patient
    FK  substance             → ActiveSubstance
    (no relationship attributes — spec doesn't define any)

----------------------------------------------------------
2.22 Shift
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    PK  shift_id           — surrogate
    FK  department         → Department
        shift_date         (DATE — for night shift = starting date, A40)
    FK  shift_slot         → ShiftSlot
    FK  status             → ShiftStatus  (DRAFT or LOCKED)

UNIQUE: (department, shift_date, shift_slot)

----------------------------------------------------------
2.23 ShiftAssignment   (M:N: Shift ↔ Staff)
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    PK  (shift, staff)     — composite
    FK  shift              → Shift
    FK  staff              → Staff (parent)

Triggers (per assumptions A41–A46):
  - R1 (3 docs, 6 nurses, 2 admins per shift): validated on
    lock_shift procedure.
  - R2 (resident → senior on same shift): validated on
    lock_shift.
  - R3/R4/R5 (monthly limits 15/20/25): BEFORE INSERT.
  - R6 (≥8h rest between consecutive shifts): BEFORE INSERT.
  - R7 (max 3 consecutive nights): BEFORE INSERT.
  - LOCKED shift modifications: blocked by trigger.

----------------------------------------------------------
2.24 HospitalizationRating
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    PK  hospitalization    → Hospitalization  (also FK; 0:1)
        nursing_quality    (1–5)
        cleanliness        (1–5)
        food               (1–5)
        overall            (1–5)
        rated_at           (DATETIME)

CHECK on each Likert column: BETWEEN 1 AND 5 (A48)
TRIGGER: requires Hospitalization.discharge_date IS NOT NULL (A49)

----------------------------------------------------------
2.25 DoctorRating
----------------------------------------------------------
Attributes:
    PK  (hospitalization, doctor)   — composite
    FK  hospitalization    → Hospitalization
    FK  doctor             → Doctor
        medical_care_quality (1–5)
        rated_at           (DATETIME)

TRIGGER: requires
   (a) Hospitalization.discharge_date IS NOT NULL, AND
   (b) EXISTS Prescription with same (hospitalization, doctor)

----------------------------------------------------------
2.26 Image entities (showcase — A52)
----------------------------------------------------------
Five parallel tables, each shaped:
    PK  image_id           — surrogate
    FK  parent             → (Doctor / Department / OperatingRoom
                              / MedicalProcedure / Drug)
        image_url
        caption
        ordering           (small int, display order)

Tables:
    DoctorImage             → Doctor
    DepartmentImage         → Department
    OperatingRoomImage      → OperatingRoom
    MedicalProcedureImage   → MedicalProcedure
    DrugImage               → Drug

Patient / Nurse / AdminStaff: NO image tables (A53).

SECTION 3 — RELATIONSHIPS (cardinality cheat sheet)
==========================================================

3.1  ISA hierarchies
     Staff ─ ISA ─ {Doctor, Nurse, AdminStaff}    disjoint, total

3.2  Many-to-many
     Doctor ─── HAS_SPECIALTY ─── Specialty
       (1,N) ─── (1,N)
       (one doctor has ≥1 specialty; a specialty can be held
        by ≥0 doctors at any time, but ≥1 in practice)

     Doctor ─── WORKS_IN ─── Department
       (1,N) ─── (1,N)
       (a doctor belongs to one or more departments;
        a department has many doctors)

     Drug ─── CONTAINS ─── ActiveSubstance
       (1,N) ─── (1,N)

     Patient ─── ALLERGIC_TO ─── ActiveSubstance
       (0,N) ─── (0,N)

     Shift ─── HAS_ASSIGNMENT ─── Staff
       (3+,N) ─── (0,N)
       (each shift, when LOCKED, has at least 11 staff —
        but during DRAFT can be incomplete; ER shows the
        general (0,N) — (0,N) and notes the rule)

     ProcedureExecution ─── HAS_ASSISTANT ─── Staff
       (0,N) ─── (0,N)
       (assistants are optional; staff must be Doctor or Nurse)

3.3  One-to-many (mandatory many side)
     Department ─── HAS_NURSE ─── Nurse
       (1,1) ─── (1,N)
       (each Nurse has exactly one Department)

     Department ─── HAS_ADMIN ─── AdminStaff
       (1,1) ─── (1,N)
       (each AdminStaff has exactly one Department)

     Department ─── HAS_BED ─── Bed              (identifying)
       (1,1) ─── (1,N)

     Patient ─── HAS_EMERGENCY_CONTACT ─── EmergencyContact   (identifying)
       (1,1) ─── (0,N)

     Patient ─── HAS_TRIAGE ─── Triage
       (1,1) ─── (0,N)

     Nurse ─── PERFORMS_TRIAGE ─── Triage
       (1,1) ─── (0,N)

     Patient ─── HAS_HOSPITALIZATION ─── Hospitalization
       (1,1) ─── (0,N)

     Bed ─── ASSIGNED_TO ─── Hospitalization
       (1,1) ─── (0,N)

     Department ─── HOSPITALIZES_IN ─── Hospitalization
       (1,1) ─── (0,N)

     Triage ─── LEADS_TO ─── Hospitalization
       (0,1) ─── (0,1)
       (a triage may or may not lead to hospitalization;
        a hospitalization may or may not have a triage)

     ICD10 ─── ADMIT_DIAG ─── Hospitalization
       (1,1) ─── (0,N)
     ICD10 ─── DISCHARGE_DIAG ─── Hospitalization
       (0,1) ─── (0,N)
       (admission diagnosis required, discharge diagnosis only
        after discharge)

     KEN ─── PRICES ─── Hospitalization
       (1,1) ─── (0,N)

     Hospitalization ─── HAS_LAB_EXAM ─── LabExam
       (1,1) ─── (0,N)

     Doctor ─── ORDERED ─── LabExam
       (1,1) ─── (0,N)

     Hospitalization ─── HAS_PROCEDURE ─── ProcedureExecution
       (1,1) ─── (0,N)

     MedicalProcedure ─── EXECUTED_AS ─── ProcedureExecution
       (1,1) ─── (0,N)

     OperatingRoom ─── HOSTS ─── ProcedureExecution
       (1,1) ─── (0,N)
       (room is mandatory only for procedures requiring a space;
        in ER drawn as (0,1) ─── (0,N) since some procedures
        don't need rooms)

     Doctor ─── PERFORMS_AS_MAIN ─── ProcedureExecution
       (1,1) ─── (0,N)

     Doctor ─── PRESCRIBED ─── Prescription
       (1,1) ─── (0,N)

     Patient ─── RECEIVES ─── Prescription
       (1,1) ─── (0,N)

     Drug ─── PRESCRIBED_AS ─── Prescription
       (1,1) ─── (0,N)

     Hospitalization ─── DURING ─── Prescription
       (1,1) ─── (0,N)

     Department ─── HAS_SHIFT ─── Shift
       (1,1) ─── (0,N)

3.4  One-to-one (or 0:1)
     Department ─── DIRECTED_BY ─── Doctor
       (1,1) ─── (0,1)
       (every department has a director; a doctor directs at
        most one department)

     Hospitalization ─── HAS_RATING ─── HospitalizationRating
       (0,1) ─── (1,1)

3.5  Recursive
     Doctor ─── SUPERVISES ─── Doctor
       supervisor side (0,N) ─── supervisee side (0,1)

SECTION 5 — NON-ER CONSTRAINTS (for SQL implementation)
==========================================================

These rules cannot be expressed in classical ER notation;
they must be enforced by CHECK / triggers / stored procedures.
List for the install.sql roadmap:

C1.  Doctor.rank = 'Resident' ⇒ supervisor IS NOT NULL    (CHECK or trigger)
C2.  Doctor.rank = 'Director' ⇒ supervisor IS NULL         (CHECK or trigger)
C3.  No cycles in Doctor.supervises chain                  (trigger, recursive)
C4.  Hospitalization.department = Bed.department           (trigger, A21)
C5.  Hospitalization.discharge_date >= admission_date      (CHECK)
C6.  Hospitalization.extra_cost computed at discharge      (trigger)
C7.  LabExam: at least one of result_text / result_numeric (CHECK, A26)
C8.  ProcedureExecution: end_time > start_time             (CHECK)
C9.  No two ProcedureExecutions in same room overlap       (trigger, A31.1)
C10. No same Doctor in two overlapping executions          (trigger, A31.2)
C11. ProcedureAssistant.staff is Doctor or Nurse           (trigger / CHECK)
C12. Prescription unique (doctor,patient,drug,start_date)  (UNIQUE constraint, A35)
C13. Prescription end_date IS NULL OR > start_date         (CHECK, A38)
C14. Prescription: drug ingredients ∉ patient.allergies    (trigger, A37)
C15. Shift uniqueness (department,date,shift_slot)         (UNIQUE)
C16. ShiftAssignment R1 (staffing minimums) at LOCK time   (procedure, A41)
C17. ShiftAssignment R2 (senior with resident) at LOCK     (procedure, A42)
C18. ShiftAssignment R3-R5 (monthly limits)                (trigger, A43)
C19. ShiftAssignment R6 (8h rest)                          (trigger, A44)
C20. ShiftAssignment R7 (3 consecutive nights max)         (trigger, A45)
C21. LOCKED shifts cannot be modified                      (trigger)
C22. Likert columns BETWEEN 1 AND 5                        (CHECK, A48)
C23. Ratings only allowed when discharge_date IS NOT NULL  (trigger, A49)
C24. DoctorRating: doctor must have prescribed in that
       hospitalization                                      (trigger, A51)

SECTION 6 — DRAWING THE ER DIAGRAM (er.pdf)
==========================================================

Recommended approach: draw.io (free, web-based) or Lucidchart.
MySQL Workbench's EER tool is NOT classical Chen ER notation
— it's relational. Use Workbench for relational.pdf, not er.pdf.

Suggested layout:
  - Top-left: Staff ISA hierarchy (Staff parent + 3 subclasses,
    triangle/d notation).
  - Top-right: Department + Bed (weak).
  - Center-left: Patient + EmergencyContact (weak) + Allergy.
  - Center: Triage → Hospitalization (the spine).
  - Bottom-left: Drug ↔ ActiveSubstance + Prescription.
  - Bottom-center: LabExam, ProcedureExecution, OperatingRoom,
    MedicalProcedure, ProcedureAssistant.
  - Bottom-right: Shift + ShiftAssignment.
  - Far-right: ratings (HospitalizationRating, DoctorRating).
  - Lookup tables in a small box / legend at the bottom.
  - Image entities clustered near their parents.

Notation used (Silberschatz 7th ed. — see N1–N11 in PART 2):
  - Rectangles for entities, double rectangles for weak.
  - Attributes listed INSIDE the entity rectangle, PK underlined.
  - Multi-valued attributes written in { } braces inside the box.
  - Derived attributes written with a slash prefix, e.g. /age.
  - Diamonds for relationships, double diamonds for identifying.
  - Relationship attributes hung off the diamond by a DASHED line.
  - Cardinality: ARROW on the entity side = "one"; plain line = "many".
  - Total participation: DOUBLE line between entity and relationship.
  - ISA: triangle with 'd' inside (disjoint), double line up to parent
    (total specialization).
  - Recursive relationships: lines connect entity to itself with role
    labels at each endpoint.

PART 2 — ASSUMPTIONS LOG
==========================================================================
==========================================================================

This is the running log of every design decision with reasoning,
captured as we worked through the ER stage layer by layer.
Each assumption has a stable ID (A1–A53) referenced from PART 1.
At the end, notation conventions (N1–N11) document the
Silberschatz 7th-edition style we follow on the ER diagram.



[A1] Lookup tables instead of ENUMs
  Where: Everywhere a fixed vocabulary appears (urgency levels
         1–5, shift slots morning/afternoon/night, doctor ranks,
         nurse ranks, admin roles, insurance types, bed types,
         bed statuses, procedure categories, staff types).
  Decision: Each becomes a small lookup ENTITY in the ER and
            a reference TABLE in SQL.
  Why: Spec forbids ENUM, JSON, arrays, XML. Lookup tables
       give referential integrity and easy extension.

[A2] Natural vs surrogate keys at conceptual level
  Where: ER diagram.
  Decision: ER shows natural keys as identifying attributes
            (AMKA, ICD-10 code, KEN code, EMA drug code,
            license_number, etc.). Surrogate vs natural is
            decided per-table at the relational stage.
  Preliminary plan:
    - Reference data (ICD10, KEN, Drug, Procedure): natural PKs
    - Everything else: surrogate id PKs

[A3] Images
  Where: Doctors, Departments, OperatingRooms, MedicalProcedures,
         Drugs (the "showcase" entities for the planned website).
  Decision: Single Image entity in ER, related to each showcase
            entity by separate relationships. In relational layer
            we'll likely use one Image table per entity type
            (clean FKs) OR an image_url column on each entity
            — to be decided later.
  Why: Spec says all entities should have associated images with
       captions for the future website.

----------------------------------------------------------
LAYER 1 — STAFF HIERARCHY
----------------------------------------------------------

[A4] Age is stored as date_of_birth  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Staff entity (all subtypes) and Patient entity.
  Decision: Store date_of_birth, derive age.
  Why: Age changes yearly; storing it as an integer means stale
       data. Standard normalization fix.
  Note: The spec literally says "ηλικία" (age). Flag in README
        as an intentional deviation for correctness.

[A5] Doctor can have multiple specialties  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Doctor subclass.
  Decision: M:N relationship Doctor ↔ Specialty.
  Why: User read of the spec — doctors in practice often have
       more than one specialty (e.g. internal medicine +
       cardiology). Modeling as M:N is more flexible than 1:N
       and handles the single-specialty case naturally.
  Implication: A separate associative entity DOCTOR_SPECIALTY
               in the relational layer.

[A6] Doctor supervision constraints are NOT structural
  Where: Doctor self-relationship SUPERVISES.
  Decision: ER models supervisor as a 0..1 self-FK on Doctor.
            The rules — "Residents must have a supervisor",
            "Directors must NOT have one", "no cyclic supervision"
            — are enforced by CHECK constraints + triggers, not
            by ER structure.
  Why: ER cannot natively express "depends on rank" or "acyclic".

[A7] ISA on Staff is disjoint + total
  Where: Staff → {Doctor, Nurse, AdminStaff}.
  Decision: Disjoint (a person is exactly one type) and total
            (every staff member is one of the three).
  Why: Spec lists the three categories as parallel and exhaustive.

LAYER 2 — DEPARTMENT & BED
----------------------------------------------------------

[A8] Department.bed_count is a DERIVED attribute  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Department.
  Decision: Compute from COUNT(beds in dept), not stored as a
            column. In ER, draw it as a dashed/derived oval.
  Why: Storing bed_count would denormalize since we already model
       Bed as its own entity tied to Department. Single source of
       truth = Bed table. At ~15 departments × ~30 beds, the
       COUNT is trivial. Avoids drift between stored count and
       actual rows.

[A9] Director ≠ Membership for doctors
  Where: Department–Doctor relationships.
  Decision: MANAGES (Department→Doctor, 1:1 each side at the
            "department has one director" level) is separate from
            WORKS_IN (Doctor↔Department, M:N membership).
  Why: A doctor can be director of one department but not be a
       member of others, in principle. We'll seed data so each
       director is also a member of the department they direct.

[A10] Nurses and AdminStaff belong to exactly ONE department
  Where: NURSE_BELONGS_TO, ADMIN_BELONGS_TO relationships.
  Decision: 1:N from Department to Nurse / AdminStaff
            (each nurse / admin → exactly 1 department; a
            department has many nurses / admins).
  Why: Spec literally says "ανήκουν σε ένα τμήμα" (belong to
       one department) for both groups.

[A11] Bed is a WEAK entity
  Where: Bed.
  Decision: Bed has partial key bed_number, identified by its
            owning Department. Identifying relationship HAS_BED.
  Why: Spec says "μοναδικό αριθμό" (unique number). Most
       reasonable reading: unique within department, not globally.

LAYER 3 — PATIENT, EMERGENCY CONTACTS, ALLERGIES
----------------------------------------------------------

[A12] Gender modeled as a lookup table  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Patient.gender.
  Decision: Lookup table Gender(code, name) with rows like
            ('M','Male'), ('F','Female'), ('O','Other').
            Patient.gender_code is a FK to it.
  Why: Consistency with the global no-ENUM policy (A1).
       Even though it's small and stable, treating it the same
       as every other category keeps the schema uniform.

[A13] Insurance provider is a lookup table  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Patient.insurance_provider.
  Decision: Lookup table InsuranceProvider(provider_id, name).
            Patient.insurance_provider_id FK to it.
  Why: Query Q1 explicitly groups by insurance provider. A
       lookup gives clean GROUP BY, prevents typos / variants
       (e.g. "ΕΦΚΑ" vs "EFKA"), and is extensible if new
       providers are added.

[A14] Emergency contact is a WEAK entity, not a JSON field
       [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Patient.
  Decision: EmergencyContact is a weak entity owned by Patient.
            Partial key = contact_seq (1, 2, ... per patient).
            Identifying relationship HAS_EMERGENCY_CONTACT.
            Attributes: full_name, relationship (free text),
            phone, email (optional).
  Why: Spec uses plural "οικείου(-ων)" — patient may have
       multiple. Multi-valued attributes must be modeled as a
       separate entity to satisfy 1NF.
  Note: relationship field is FREE TEXT (not a lookup) —
        the variation (mother, husband, neighbor, friend, ...)
        is too high to enumerate cleanly.

[A15] Allergies are recorded against ActiveSubstance, not Drug
       [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Patient ↔ ActiveSubstance.
  Decision: M:N relationship ALLERGIC_TO between Patient and
            ActiveSubstance. No relationship attributes.
            NOT modeled as Patient ↔ Drug.
  Why: Spec explicitly says "αλλεργίες σε δραστικές ουσίες"
       (allergies to active substances). Cross-reactions
       across different drugs sharing the same active substance
       must be caught by the prescription check.
  Note: Earlier draft included a recorded_date attribute on
        the relationship — removed because the spec does not
        mention it. Adding speculative attributes is out of
        scope for the project.

[A16] EMA Article 57 active-substance loading uses '|' split
       [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Drug ↔ ActiveSubstance population step.
  Decision: When loading the EMA file, split each drug's
            active-substance field on '|' and insert one row
            per substance into the M:N table.
  Why: Spec footnote tells us so explicitly. Noted now so we
       don't forget at data-loading time.

[A17] Allergy/prescription matching uses BOTH normalization
       AND LIKE  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Trigger preventing prescription of a drug containing
         an allergen.
  Decision: At load time, normalize active-substance names
            (strip salts/forms, lowercase, trim). At check
            time in the trigger, also use LIKE for partial
            match. Belt and suspenders.
  Why: Spec says either approach is acceptable. Doing both
       maximizes the chance of catching real allergic conflicts.
       Cost: occasional false positive (we'd refuse a safe drug)
       — acceptable trade-off in a clinical setting.

LAYER 4 — TRIAGE & HOSPITALIZATION
----------------------------------------------------------

[A18] Triage is a standalone entity, NOT an attribute of
       Hospitalization  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Triage entity.
  Decision: Triage is its own entity. Hospitalization
            optionally references the Triage that led to it
            (FK nullable).
  Why: Spec says some triaged patients are sent home — they
       have a triage record but no hospitalization. So Triage
       must be able to exist without a Hospitalization.

[A19] Triage wait_time is a DERIVED attribute  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Triage.
  Decision: wait_time = triage_time − arrival_time. Not stored.
            Drawn as a dashed oval in ER.
  Why: Q15 asks for "μέσο χρόνο αναμονής ανά επίπεδο". Avoids
       drift; both timestamps are stored explicitly.

[A20] Triage outcome is a lookup table  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Triage.outcome.
  Decision: Lookup table TriageOutcome(code, name) with rows
            for "discharged with instructions" and "referred
            for hospitalization". Extensible (e.g. LWBS,
            transferred) if needed.
  Why: Same justification as A1 — fixed vocabulary, no enums.

[A21] Hospitalization.department is stored explicitly
       (denormalized) with consistency trigger  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Hospitalization.
  Decision: Store department FK on Hospitalization even though
            it is derivable via Hospitalization.bed → Bed.dept.
            A trigger / CHECK enforces equality.
  Why: Many queries (Q1, Q3, Q12, Q15) group by hospitalization
       department. Explicit FK keeps queries simpler and faster
       and clarifies the ER. Trigger prevents drift.

[A22] total_cost is FROZEN at discharge time, stored, not
       derived live  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Hospitalization.
  Decision: Cost is computed at discharge from KEN parameters
            in effect at that time, then stored. Not recomputed
            from KEN if KEN data changes later.
  Why: A hospitalization's cost should be auditable — KEN base
       costs and surcharge rates may change over time, but a
       past hospitalization's bill is fixed.

[A23] Hospitalization stores base_cost AND extra_cost
       separately, total is derived  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Hospitalization cost columns.
  Decision: Two stored columns: base_cost (from KEN.base_cost
            at discharge) and extra_cost (surcharge for days
            beyond ΜΔΝ; 0 if stay within ΜΔΝ).
            total_cost = base_cost + extra_cost (derived).
  Why: Q1 explicitly asks for "βασικό κόστος έναντι πρόσθετης
       χρέωσης λόγω υπέρβασης ΜΔΝ" broken out — storing them
       separately makes that a clean SUM, no re-decomposition.

[A24] ICD10.category is stored, computed at load time
       [CONFIRMED]
  Where: ICD10 reference table.
  Decision: At load time, extract the category prefix from
            each ICD-10 code (e.g. "I21.0" → "I" / circulatory)
            and store as a separate column.
  Why: Q14 groups by ICD-10 *category*, not full code.
       Computing the prefix once at load avoids repeated string
       work at query time and lets us index on category.

[A25] Daily surcharge rate is a per-KEN column with a
       sensible default formula  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: KEN reference table.
  Decision: Add daily_surcharge_rate column on KEN. If the
            source data doesn't specify a rate per KEN, default
            it to base_cost / avg_length_of_stay (one extra day
            costs the average per-day rate of that KEN).
            Document the formula in README.
  Why: Spec says "αναλογική πρόσθετη ημερήσια χρέωση"
       (proportional additional daily charge) but doesn't
       commit to a rate. Per-KEN is more flexible than a global
       constant; the default formula is documented and replaceable.

----------------------------------------------------------
LAYER 5 — LAB EXAMS, PROCEDURES, OPERATING ROOMS,
          SURGICAL ASSISTANTS
----------------------------------------------------------

[A26] LabExam result has both text and numeric+unit columns,
       at least one required  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: LabExam.
  Decision: Three nullable columns: result_text, result_numeric,
            result_unit. CHECK constraint: at least one of
            result_text or result_numeric must be non-null.
  Why: Spec: "αποτέλεσμα (κείμενο ή/και αριθμητική τιμή με
       μονάδα μέτρησης)" — text and/or numeric. Captures all
       three real cases (text only, numeric only, both).

[A27] LabExam is linked to Hospitalization, not directly
       to Patient  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: LabExam.hospitalization FK.
  Decision: Mandatory FK from LabExam to Hospitalization.
            Patient is reachable via Hospitalization.patient.
  Why: Spec: "Κάθε νοσηλεία μπορεί να συνδέεται με
       εργαστηριακές εξετάσεις". Outpatient labs are out
       of scope.

[A28] MedicalProcedure (catalog) and ProcedureExecution
       (event) are two distinct entities  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: MedicalProcedure vs ProcedureExecution.
  Decision: MedicalProcedure = catalog of procedure types
            (loaded from Greek codification, ~150 entries).
            ProcedureExecution = a specific performance during
            a hospitalization, with room, surgeon, times.
  Why: Standard catalog/event split. Same procedure type can
       be executed many times across many hospitalizations.

[A29] OperatingRoom is standalone, NOT tied to a Department
       [CONFIRMED]
  Where: OperatingRoom entity.
  Decision: OperatingRoom has no FK to Department.
  Why: An OR / procedure room typically serves multiple
       departments (cardiology + general surgery share theaters).
       Tying a room to one department would be artificial.

[A30] ProcedureExecution stores start_time AND end_time
       as DATETIMEs  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: ProcedureExecution.
  Decision: Store start_time and end_time independently, not
            (start_time + duration).
  Why: The two anti-conflict rules (A31) require interval
       comparisons. Real procedures over/underrun catalog
       duration, so a stored end_time is the only honest model.

[A31] Two ProcedureExecution anti-conflict rules, enforced
       as triggers  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: ProcedureExecution + ProcedureAssistant.
  Decision: Two trigger-enforced rules:
    (1) No two executions in the SAME room with overlapping
        [start_time, end_time] intervals.
    (2) No two executions in which the SAME doctor (as
        main_surgeon or as assistant) appears with overlapping
        intervals.
  Why: Not expressible in pure ER. Will be enforced as BEFORE
       INSERT / BEFORE UPDATE triggers raising an error on
       overlap.

[A32] ProcedureExecution.actual_cost is frozen at execution
       time  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: ProcedureExecution.actual_cost.
  Decision: Stored on the execution row. Defaults to
            MedicalProcedure.standard_cost at insert time
            but can be overridden.
  Why: Same reasoning as A22 — the catalog price may change;
       the bill for a specific execution is fixed.

[A33] ProcedureAssistant FK points to Staff parent, with a
       check that staff is Doctor or Nurse  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: ProcedureAssistant.staff FK.
  Decision: FK references Staff (the ISA parent). A trigger /
            CHECK enforces: the staff row's type is Doctor
            or Nurse (not AdminStaff).
  Why: Spec says assistants can be doctors OR nurses. Single
       FK to Staff parent is cleaner than two mutually-exclusive
       FK columns. Justifies having modeled Staff as a unified
       parent in the ISA (A7).

LAYER 6 — DRUGS & PRESCRIPTIONS
----------------------------------------------------------

[A34] Drug PK is a surrogate id; EMA code is a separate
       UNIQUE column  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Drug reference table.
  Decision: drug_id (surrogate INT PK). ema_code stored as
            a separate column with UNIQUE constraint.
  Why: EMA product identifiers are long, sometimes versioned,
       and same brand may appear under multiple identifiers.
       A surrogate keeps FK columns small (Prescription,
       Drug↔ActiveSubstance) while still preserving the
       natural identifier for traceability.

[A35] Prescription has surrogate PK + UNIQUE composite key
       on (doctor, patient, drug, start_date)  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Prescription.
  Decision: prescription_id surrogate PK. UNIQUE constraint
            on (doctor_id, patient_id, drug_id, start_date).
  Why: Spec literally requires "Κάθε συνδυασμός (ιατρός,
       ασθενής, φάρμακο, ημερομηνία έναρξης) είναι μοναδικός".
       Surrogate PK simplifies FK references from rating
       tables and other places.

[A36] Prescription is mandatorily tied to a Hospitalization
       [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Prescription.hospitalization FK.
  Decision: NOT NULL FK from Prescription to Hospitalization.
  Why: Q4 and Q6 only make sense if prescriptions are scoped
       to a hospitalization (rating "the doctors who
       prescribed during the hospitalization"). Outpatient
       prescriptions are out of scope per our reading of the
       spec.

[A37] Allergy check on Prescription INSERT is enforced by
       trigger  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: BEFORE INSERT trigger on Prescription.
  Decision: Trigger fetches all active substances of the
            drug being prescribed, checks each against the
            patient's allergy list using the matching strategy
            from A17 (normalized name + LIKE). On match, raise
            an error and abort the insert.
  Why: Spec: "Απαγορεύεται η συνταγογράφηση φαρμάκου αν
       κάποια από τις δραστικές ουσίες του βρίσκεται στις
       αλλεργίες του ασθενή". This is the core data-integrity
       rule of the medication subsystem; it cannot be expressed
       structurally in ER.

[A38] Prescription end_date is nullable; CHECK
       end_date IS NULL OR end_date > start_date  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Prescription.end_date.
  Decision: end_date NULLABLE. CHECK constraint:
            end_date IS NULL OR end_date > start_date.
  Why: Real prescriptions can be open-ended ("until further
       notice"). NULL = still active. CHECK ensures non-null
       values are after start.

LAYER 7 — SHIFTS / DUTY ROSTER
----------------------------------------------------------

[A39] Shift is its own entity; ShiftAssignment is a separate
       M:N relationship  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Shift, ShiftAssignment.
  Decision: Shift = (department, shift_date, shift_slot) with
            UNIQUE on the triple. ShiftAssignment is the M:N
            associative entity between Shift and Staff with
            PK (shift_id, staff_id).
  Why: A shift exists as a "duty slot" independent of any
       specific staff being assigned, and it carries its own
       state (A41). Modeling it as a 4-way relationship would
       make R1/R2 awkward to express.

[A40] Night-shift calendar date = the date the shift STARTS
       [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Shift.shift_date semantics for shift_slot = night.
  Decision: A night shift dated 2026-05-10 runs
            2026-05-10 23:00 → 2026-05-11 07:00.
  Why: Unambiguous, simplifies the "consecutive nights" rule
       (A45) — N consecutive nights = consecutive shift_date
       values with shift_slot = night. Document explicitly
       since this affects rest-period calculation (A44).

[A41] R1 (≥3 doctors, ≥6 nurses, ≥2 admins per shift) is
       enforced via a DRAFT / LOCKED Shift.status, not per
       insert  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Shift.status lookup, lock-shift stored procedure.
  Decision: Add Shift.status ∈ {DRAFT, LOCKED} (lookup table).
            Inserting/removing assignments is free while
            DRAFT. A stored procedure lock_shift(shift_id)
            verifies the staffing minimums (R1) and the
            resident-with-senior rule (R2, see A42), and only
            then sets status = LOCKED. Once LOCKED, any
            modification is rejected by trigger.
  Why: R1 is a completeness rule, not a per-row rule. You
       can't reject the first doctor inserted into a shift
       just because there's only one doctor so far. DRAFT/
       LOCKED matches real-world rostering workflows
       (drafted, reviewed, approved).

[A42] R2 (resident → at least one Curator A or Director on
       the same shift) is enforced at LOCK time  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: lock_shift stored procedure.
  Decision: Same enforcement path as R1. Validation runs as
            part of lock_shift. Once LOCKED, removing a
            Curator A / Director that R2 depends on is
            blocked by trigger (or requires unlocking).
  Why: Same completeness flavor as R1 — checking on each
       insert is awkward because the senior may be added
       later in the drafting session.

[A43] R3–R5 (monthly limits 15 / 20 / 25) enforced by BEFORE
       INSERT trigger on ShiftAssignment  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: ShiftAssignment trigger.
  Decision: On INSERT, look up the staff's StaffType, count
            existing assignments for that staff in the same
            calendar month, and reject if the count would
            reach the limit (15 doctors / 20 nurses / 25
            admins).
  Why: Monthly limits are per-row rules — exactly the case
       triggers handle well. The "calendar month" matches
       YEAR(shift_date), MONTH(shift_date).

[A44] R6 (≥8 hours rest between consecutive shifts) enforced
       by BEFORE INSERT trigger using a helper that returns
       a shift's [start_dt, end_dt] interval  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: ShiftAssignment trigger + helper function.
  Decision: Helper _shift_window(shift_id) returns the full
            DATETIME interval, accounting for night shifts
            crossing midnight (per A40). Trigger fetches the
            staff's neighbouring assignments (closest before
            and after the new shift's window) and verifies
            both gaps are ≥ 8 hours.
  Why: Need real DATETIMEs because the rule cuts across
       calendar days (e.g. afternoon→morning next day = 16h
       gap, fine; night→morning same person two-day cycle
       could fail).

[A45] R7 (max 3 consecutive night shifts) enforced by BEFORE
       INSERT trigger  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: ShiftAssignment trigger (when shift_slot = night).
  Decision: On insert into a night shift dated D, check
            whether the staff is already assigned to night
            shifts on D-1, D-2, D-3. If all three exist, the
            new insert would make 4-in-a-row → reject.
  Why: "Consecutive" is well-defined under A40 (night shifts
       indexed by their starting date). Three lookups; cheap.

[A46] All shift-related trigger errors use SIGNAL with
       descriptive SQLSTATE and human-readable message
       [CONFIRMED]
  Where: All shift triggers and the lock_shift procedure.
  Decision: Each violation raises a SIGNAL with a stable
            SQLSTATE (e.g. '45R03' for R3 monthly limit) and
            a message identifying the rule and the offending
            row.
  Why: The application layer can match on SQLSTATE for
       i18n / user-friendly errors; debugging the schema is
       easier when triggers fail loudly with named rules.

LAYER 8 — RATINGS & IMAGES
----------------------------------------------------------

[A47] HospitalizationRating is 0:1 with Hospitalization
       (one rating max, no revisions)  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: HospitalizationRating.
  Decision: PK = hospitalization_id (also FK). A patient
            can submit at most one rating per hospitalization.
  Why: Spec implies a single post-discharge evaluation.
       Revisions are out of scope.

[A48] Likert columns enforced by CHECK 1–5, not lookup
       [CONFIRMED]
  Where: HospitalizationRating, DoctorRating.
  Decision: Each rating column is TINYINT with
            CHECK (value BETWEEN 1 AND 5).
  Why: Pure numeric scale with self-evident semantics —
       a lookup table would be ceremony for no benefit.
       This is the one place we deviate from the
       lookup-everywhere policy (A1) deliberately.

[A49] Rating insert requires Hospitalization.discharge_date
       IS NOT NULL  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: BEFORE INSERT triggers on HospitalizationRating
         and DoctorRating.
  Decision: Trigger checks discharge_date is set; otherwise
            raises an error.
  Why: Spec: "Αξιολόγηση επιτρέπεται μόνο σε ασθενείς με
       ολοκληρωμένη νοσηλεία".

[A50] DoctorRating PK = (hospitalization, doctor)
       [CONFIRMED]
  Where: DoctorRating.
  Decision: Composite PK. A patient can rate the same doctor
            multiple times across multiple hospitalizations,
            but at most once per hospitalization.
  Why: Spec scopes the rating to the hospitalization context.
       Keying on (patient, doctor) would prevent a returning
       patient from rating the same doctor again — wrong.

[A51] DoctorRating trigger validates the doctor actually
       prescribed during that hospitalization  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: BEFORE INSERT trigger on DoctorRating.
  Decision: Trigger verifies EXISTS a Prescription with
            matching (hospitalization, doctor); otherwise
            raises an error. Plus the A49 discharge check.
  Why: Spec: "κάθε ιατρό που συνταγογράφησε κατά τη
       νοσηλεία του".

[A52] One Image table per showcase entity (no polymorphic
       table)  [CONFIRMED]
  Where: DoctorImage, DepartmentImage, OperatingRoomImage,
         MedicalProcedureImage, DrugImage.
  Decision: Five tables, each with (parent_id FK,
            image_url, caption, ordering). Multiple images
            per parent allowed.
  Why: Polymorphic FKs aren't enforceable in MySQL — losing
       referential integrity is a bigger cost than five
       extra small tables. Clear, queryable, indexable.

[A53] Patient / Nurse / AdminStaff images are OUT of scope
       [CONFIRMED]
  Where: Image coverage decision.
  Decision: No image tables for Patient (privacy), Nurse, or
            AdminStaff (not public-facing on the website).
            Doctor images are in scope (public profiles).
  Why: Spec talks about the website needing images of
       departments, doctors, equipment — not patients or
       all staff. Privacy + minimalism principle.

NOTATION CONVENTIONS (Silberschatz 7th ed., per professor's
slides)
----------------------------------------------------------

[N1] Entities drawn as rectangles with header bar; attributes
     listed inside, PK underlined.
[N2] Weak entities: double-line rectangle.
[N3] Relationships: diamonds, name in italics. Plain lines
     between diamond and entity.
[N4] Cardinality shown by ARROWS on the entity side of a
     relationship line. Arrow = "one"; plain line = "many".
     (No (min,max) labels.)
[N5] Total participation: DOUBLE line between entity and
     relationship.
[N6] Relationship attributes: small rectangle attached to the
     diamond by a DASHED line.
[N7] Multi-valued attributes: written inside the entity in
     braces, e.g. { day, start_time, end_time }.
[N8] Derived attributes: slash prefix, e.g. /age.
[N9] Composite attributes: indented sub-attributes inside the
     entity box.
[N10] ISA: triangle labeled "ISA" with "d" inside (disjoint).
      Double line from parent to triangle for total
      specialization.
[N11] Recursive relationships: relationship line connects
      entity to itself with role labels at each endpoint.
